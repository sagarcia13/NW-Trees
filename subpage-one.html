BIG LEAF MAPLE

LOCATION: Pacific coast

HEIGHT: 30’-75’

AGE: 200 years

DESCRIPTION:Although Big-Leaf Maple will grow on drier sites, it is often found along stream banks and does best on similarly moist sites—its leaves will grow bigger and more impressive when growing in moist, shady areas.

Big-Leaf Maple is easily recognizable by its large 5-lobed leaves–often 12-inches (30cm) across, but sometimes much smaller– 6” (15cm). In the spring, greenish-yellow flower clusters emerge before or with the leaves. The seeds are the typical paired, winged samaras or “helicopter seeds” of maple trees.

Although this tree may be too big for a small yard, it is impressive in larger yards and parks. It effectively creates a shady and cool environment beneath its leafy branches, an ideal place for a picnic on a hot, summer day. In fall, the leaves turn yellow, turning brown after falling, creating a carpet of large, brown leaves persisting through the winter.
